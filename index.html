About Me

I'm a Senior Data Scientist at Columbia University's Robert N. Butler Aging Center, where I study biological aging.

I design hierarchical mixed-effects models to characterize aging trajectories across a broad range of biomarkers, and build machine learning models that compress these trajectories into aging measures that can be estimated from a single blood test.

These tools have direct applications in personalized medicine and clinical trial design. They serve as sensitive endpoints to detect whether an intervention is actually slowing the aging process.

This work has yielded a patented and licensed algorithm, with several publications in progress.

Outside of research, I build software. Tools to accelerate my own work, contributions to open-source projects I love, and experiments with new frameworks.
