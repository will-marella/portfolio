About

I'm a data scientist at Columbia University's Mailman School of Public Health, where I study biological aging. I construct longitudinal biomarkers of aging that serve as trial endpoints to better measure whether interventions effectively slow the aging process.

I build and validate machine learning models that predict aging biomarkers from molecular data, enabling biological age and pace of aging to be estimated from a single blood test. This work has led to a patented algorithm licensed to a longevity diagnostics company.

I also hold a Visiting Researcher appointment at the University of Cambridge and an MPhil in Population Health Sciences. Previously, I studied Biology at Brown University.

On the side, I build software to make research easier, solve my own problems, or make games and have fun.
